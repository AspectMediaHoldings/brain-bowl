# BSA Troop 3 Operations

## Purpose

Nathan is Assistant Scoutmaster for Scouts BSA Troop 3 in Marianna, FL, chartered
through Alaflo Council (Dothan, AL). This skill governs all troop operational work:
curriculum development, event planning, camp logistics, design assets, and volunteer
SOPs. It ensures consistent output aligned with BSA standards and local troop identity.

## When to Use

- Writing merit badge curriculum or rank advancement materials
- Planning fundraiser logistics (pancake breakfasts, Farmers Market events)
- Building Klondike sled designs, specs, or build checklists
- Planning Camp Alaflo events or infrastructure (Starlink setup, site logistics)
- Designing troop logos, patches, or visual identity assets
- Drafting volunteer SOPs or leader guides
- Any task referencing Troop 3, Alaflo Council, or BSA program at the unit level

## Inputs

Confirm before starting:
1. Task type: curriculum, event planning, logistics, design, or SOP
2. Audience: Scouts (specify rank), leaders, parents, or council
3. Deliverable format: Word document, SVG, checklist, script, or slide deck
4. Timeline or event date if applicable

If audience is unspecified, default to adult leaders or Assistant Scoutmaster use.

## Procedure

### Merit Badge and Curriculum Development

1. Confirm merit badge name and BSA pamphlet requirements before drafting any content.
2. Align all requirements to current BSA pamphlet — do not substitute or add requirements.
3. For Wilderness Survival: include 10 C's of Survival framework and situational awareness components.
4. Format curriculum as step-by-step leader guide with time estimates per activity.
5. Flag any requirements that need a signed-off counselor vs. unit leader.

### Event Planning (Fundraisers, Pancake Breakfasts)

1. Capture: date, location, expected attendance, menu, equipment needed, volunteer roles.
2. Produce a run-of-show with time blocks and named responsible parties.
3. Include a shopping list or bill of materials with estimated costs.
4. Include a post-event cleanup and close-out checklist.

### Klondike Sled Builds

1. Capture: target event date, number of sleds, Scout ages, available tools and materials.
2. Produce a bill of materials with dimensions and sourcing (Home Depot / Lowe's preferred for Marianna area).
3. Include a step-by-step build sequence with safety notes for youth participation.
4. Note Florida climate constraint: humidity and heat affect material selection. Prefer treated lumber or PVC over untreated wood for anything stored outdoors.

### Camp Alaflo Logistics

1. For Starlink: confirm site coordinates, power source, and mounting solution before specifying hardware.
2. For camp events (e.g., Survivor series): produce a run-of-show, station map, and leader assignment sheet.
3. All camp documents must include emergency contact and first aid station location.

### Logo and Design Assets

Troop 3 visual identity standards:
- Color palette: charcoal (#333), deep green (#182E19), gold (#C9A227)
- Identity elements: Florida Caverns arch, gopher tortoise, longleaf pines, campfire
- Typography: Bebas Neue for display, clean sans-serif for body
- Formats required: SVG (scalable for embroidery and print), PNG (digital use)
- Patches: BSA patches go on the right shirt pocket. Confirm placement before producing patch-ready art.

For new design requests: produce at least two concepts before asking Nathan to select.

### SOPs and Leader Guides

1. Format as numbered checklist with decision points clearly marked.
2. State any BSA national standards (YPT, health forms, two-deep leadership) as hard requirements — not suggestions.
3. Include a "what to do if" section for common edge cases.

## Output Format

- Curriculum: Word document (.docx), section headers per requirement, time estimates.
- Event plans: Word document with run-of-show table, shopping list, and checklist.
- Build guides: Word document with bill of materials table and numbered steps.
- Design assets: SVG primary, PNG secondary, HTML preview optional.
- SOPs: Word document or plain Markdown, numbered steps, decision points highlighted.

## Quality Checks

Before delivering any BSA document:
- Confirm all BSA requirements cited match current pamphlet language.
- Confirm two-deep leadership and YPT references are present in any leader-facing SOP.
- Confirm patch placement (right shirt pocket) if the deliverable involves patch art.
- Confirm Florida climate constraints are addressed in any outdoor build or storage document.

Before delivering design assets:
- Confirm SVG is clean and scalable (no raster embeds).
- Confirm color values match the Troop 3 palette.
- Confirm gopher tortoise, Florida Caverns, and longleaf pine elements are present in any full logo treatment.
