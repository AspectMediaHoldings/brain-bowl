# NRA Basic Range Safety Officer — Test B Answer Key

Source: RSO course presentation slides. Correct answers and source slides documented.

## Core Rules to Know Cold

- Anyone may call cease fire (Slide 41)
- RSO maintains order on the range and supervises shooting per SOPs (Slide 20)
- Most important firearm safety element: Attitude (Slide 9)
- NRA Safe Gun Handling Rule 1: Always keep gun pointed in a safe direction
- Always know your target and what is beyond it — check before every shot (Slide 38)
- When in doubt about range rules: refer to range SOPs (Slide 46)
- Eye and hearing protection, food/drink, smoking: all regulated by range SOPs

## Key Definitions

- Hangfire: Perceptible delay in ignition after primer is struck. Wait minimum 2 minutes before attempting to refire. Clear flash channel and recap.
- Squib load: Less than normal pressure after ignition; bullet may or may not exit barrel. Treat as bore obstruction.
- Misfire: Does not fire at all.
- Stoppage: Unintentional interruption in the cycle of operation (stovepipe, double feed, cylinder failure).

## Procedures

Cease fire command: Stop immediately, remain in place, wait for RSO instructions.

Serious injury/illness: Cease fire, take charge, render first aid, call 911, direct emergency personnel, get witness statements.

Shooters going forward of firing line: Only after all firearms are unloaded and grounded or benched AND RSO allows movement.

Passing a firearm: Unload, engage safety, leave action open, pass action-open facing the receiver so they confirm it is unloaded.

Unfamiliar firearm with stoppage: (1) Ask for owners manual. (2) Ask if anyone on range knows the firearm. (3) Point downrange, fire until empty, inspect clear. In that order.

## Range Safety Briefing Contents
Purpose of event, range orientation (ready area, firing line, limits, impact area), safety rules, firing line commands, emergency procedures.

## RSO Requirements
- Age 21 or older
- Full 9-hour attendance at course
- 90% or higher on examination
- Skills demonstration
- Complete application at NRAinstructors.org

## CRSO vs RSO
CRSO has additional training to develop SOPs and conduct RSO courses.
