# Dogs and Coffee — T-Shirt Sayings Skill

## Brand Identity

Dogs and Coffee is a product line under Aspect Media Holdings, LLC. The brand
voice is built on three pillars:

1. Deep love for a specific dog breed
2. Dependence on coffee
3. Sarcastic, humorous preference for dogs and coffee over most people

The humor is warm and self-aware — not mean-spirited. The buyer sees themselves
in it. Dog owners recognize it immediately.

## Design Styles and Their Saying Needs

Three design styles are in production. Sayings serve each differently:

- **Minimalist** — Text carries the design. Saying must be punchy and complete on its own. Front print.
- **Line art** — Text supports the illustration. Short tagline or identity phrase. Front print.
- **Photorealistic** — Art does the heavy lifting. Saying is a secondary tagline. Front or back. Shorter is better here.

## Saying Categories

Generate sayings in three categories for each breed:

**1. Humor** — Funny, self-deprecating, breed-specific observation. The dog does
something ridiculous. The owner is lovingly resigned to it.

**2. Identity** — Proud ownership statement. Breed personality captured in one
line. Often uses the "Mom" or "Dad" framing. Emotional, not comedic.

**3. Dogs + Coffee + No People (core brand voice)** — This is the Dogs and Coffee
signature. All three elements must appear: the specific breed, coffee, and a
sarcastic/humorous statement about preferring dogs and coffee to most people.
This is what differentiates the brand from every other pet shirt seller.

## Format Rules

**Front print** (4–10 words, reads at a glance, works on a moving person):
- Three-part rhythm works well: "Strong coffee. Loyal Rottweiler. Minimal human contact."
- Pairs work well: "Black coffee. Black Lab. That's the whole personality."
- Single punchy statement: "My GSD judges people so I don't have to."
- Maximum ~10 words. If it takes more than two seconds to read on a passing stranger, it's back-print territory.

**Back print** (15–35 words, storytelling, reads when someone walks away):
- Can be a full thought or two-sentence bit
- Works well for the "Dogs + Coffee + No People" category where the joke needs room to land
- Also valuable for TikTok content — people film themselves reading funny back-print shirts

## Breed-Specific Considerations

Each breed has personality traits that make the best sayings specific, not generic.
Use these when writing:

| Breed | Key Traits to Use |
|---|---|
| Long Hair Chihuahua | Tiny but fierce, fluffy appearance vs. feral personality, "don't let the floof fool you" angle |
| Short Hair Chihuahua | Small dog huge ego, often compared to larger breeds, smooth/sleek contrast with big attitude |
| German Shepherd | Size (90 lbs), protective instinct, judges strangers, selective listening, the "lapdog who doesn't know he's huge" angle |
| French Bulldog | Snoring, expensive vet bills, dramatic personality, bat ears and wrinkles as physical identifiers, high maintenance |
| Rottweiler | Size (110+ lbs), intimidating to others but baby to owner, "keeps people away" as a feature not a bug |
| Yellow Labrador | Loves everyone (contrast with owner who doesn't), always hungry, blonde fur everywhere, two speeds: hungry and asleep |
| Black Labrador | Shadow metaphor (follows everywhere), dark roast coffee pairing, "sad eyes" manipulation, unconditional love |

## Output Format

For each breed requested, produce:

**Front Print Sayings (6–8 options)**
- 2–3 Humor
- 2–3 Identity
- 2–3 Dogs + Coffee + No People

**Back Print Sayings (4–5 options)**
- Focus on Dogs + Coffee + No People — these have the most room to breathe
- Can include 1–2 extended humor sayings

Label each saying as [Humor], [Identity], or [Coffee+Dog+No People].
Flag sayings that work especially well for TikTok content.

## Quality Check Before Delivering

Before presenting sayings, verify each one:

- Does it name or clearly imply the specific breed? (Generic "dog" sayings fail)
- Is it under 10 words for front print?
- Does the Dogs + Coffee + No People category include all three elements?
- Would a stranger reading it on a shirt immediately understand it without context?
- Is the tone warm and self-aware — not actually mean or bitter?
