**Camping Packing List**

***(Car Camping)***

Sleeping Bag

Pillow

Blanket (if weather calls for it or if you are a cold sleeper)

Flashlight or headlamp

Pocket knife (if you have earned your Totem’ Chip)

Bowl or Plate

Drinking cup

Utensils (Fork and/or spoon)

Refillable water bottle

Hat (optional – but if it is supposed to be cold, a beanie might help you stay warmer)

Rain gear – i.e., poncho, rain jacket, etc. (A Scout is always prepared)

Large pack or duffle bag (to pack your things in)

Change of clothes (how much depends on how many nights we are camping)

Sleeping clothes (optional\* – if you are a cold sleeper, you may want some long pants and sleeves)

Toilet paper (always a good idea to bring a partial role from the house with you. Don’t depend on someone else to have some for you.)

Bag chair (optional)

Tents are provided for Scouts by the Troop. When camping, Scouts use the buddy system. They will tent with another Scout who is no more than 2 years apart in age. Those Scouts will tent together for the entire campout.

\*NOTE – Optional for sleeping clothes does not mean you have a choice to wear clothes to sleep in or not. It simply means that you may bring a set of clothes, just for sleeping in. Everyone will have some kind of clothing on when sleeping. Any questions about this, please feel free to ask an adult.

Parents (and Scouts),

We would ask that you let the Scouts pack their own gear. There are 2 core reasons behind this. First, it teaches them to be responsible for making sure they have what they need and not be dependent on someone else to do it for them. Second, when (yes, when) they forget something, they learn that they CAN do without it. But they also tend to not forget that item again.
