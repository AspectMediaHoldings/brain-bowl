# Youth Safety Guidelines — Scouting America

Source: Scouting America training materials (Google Drive)

## Barriers to Abuse

Mandatory policies required at all Scouting activities:

- Two-deep leadership at all events (minimum two registered adults, one must be 21+)
- No one-on-one contact between adults and youth
- Buddy system required on all outings
- Appropriate tenting and shower arrangements for overnight activities

## Prohibited Digital Communication
- Adults may not privately message youth via personal social media or messaging apps
- No recording of youth in private spaces

## Bullying Prevention

Types: verbal, social, physical, cyber. Bullying contradicts Scouting values.

Adult response: Intervene immediately, document the incident, report to Scoutmaster, involve parents.

## Online Safety

Threats to address: sextortion, sexting, cyberbullying, exposure to explicit content, grooming.

Resources: NCMEC, Know to Protect tip line.

## Mandated Reporting

All adult volunteers are mandated reporters.

1. Take the disclosure seriously. Do not promise confidentiality.
2. Remain calm. Do not investigate independently.
3. Report immediately to Scouting Americas 24-hour hotline and local authorities (911 if imminent danger).
4. Document what was said using the youths own words.

## S.A.F.E. Framework

- Supervision: two-deep leadership, buddy system
- Awareness: know signs of abuse and unsafe situations
- Fitness: physical and emotional readiness for activities
- Equipment: proper gear, age-appropriate guidelines per Guide to Safe Scouting
