# Scouting — Troop 3 Marianna

## Purpose

This skill governs all work Nathan does in his role as Assistant Scoutmaster for
Scouts BSA Troop 3. It encodes troop identity, council structure, BSA program
standards, and output conventions for troop communications and planning documents.

## When to Use

- Planning troop meetings, campouts, or service projects
- Tracking or documenting Scout advancement
- Drafting parent or troop communications
- Producing any document in Nathan's capacity as Assistant Scoutmaster
- Answering questions about BSA requirements, ranks, or merit badges

## Troop Reference

**Unit:** Scouts BSA Troop 3 Marianna
**Location:** Marianna, Florida
**Council:** Alaflo Council, Dothan, Alabama
**Nathan's role:** Assistant Scoutmaster

**BSA uniform patch placement:**
Troop numeral and unit patches go on the right shirt pocket. Not the left.
Do not get this wrong in any communication or instruction.

## Inputs

Before starting, confirm:

- Task type: planning, advancement, communication, or reference lookup
- Which Scouts are involved (if advancement-related)
- Event date and location (if planning-related)
- Whether the output is internal (leader-facing) or external (parent or Scout-facing)

For advancement questions: confirm the Scout's current rank and what requirement
is being addressed before providing guidance. BSA requirements change by edition.
Flag if the information may be out of date.

## Procedure

1. Confirm task type and output audience.
2. For planning tasks: identify date, location, required equipment, and any
   permit or registration requirements. Flag weather or logistics constraints
   relevant to Marianna, Florida.
3. For advancement tracking: list the specific requirement text, the Scout's
   current status, and what is needed to complete it. Do not mark anything
   complete that Nathan has not confirmed.
4. For parent or troop communications: use plain language, active voice, and
   short sentences. Friendly tone — not stiff. Include date, location, what
   to bring, and a contact for questions.
5. For BSA reference lookups: cite the source (handbook edition or BSA publication).
   Flag any requirement that may have changed since training data cutoff.
6. Run the pre-delivery check before presenting any external communication.

## Output Format

- Meeting agendas: numbered steps with time allocations
- Trip planning documents: sections for Logistics, Equipment, Roles, Safety
- Parent communications: plain prose, friendly tone, under one page
- Advancement records: table with Scout name, rank, requirement, status, date

## Quality Checks

Before delivering any output:

- Patch placement is correct: right shirt pocket, not left
- BSA requirements cited with source and edition
- Any requirement that may be out of date is flagged with [VERIFY]
- Parent communications include date, location, what to bring, and a contact
- No advancement marked complete without Nathan's confirmation
