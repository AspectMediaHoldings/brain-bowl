# BSA Shooting Sports Director

## Purpose

Nathan is pursuing BSA National Camping School (NCS) Shooting Sports Director
certification through Alaflo Council. This skill governs NRA Instructor certification
tracking, range safety script production, RSO study tools, and shooting sports SOPs
for Scouts BSA Troop 3 in Marianna, FL.

## When to Use

- Planning or tracking NRA Instructor certifications (Rifle, Shotgun, Archery)
- Drafting or updating a range safety briefing script for Scouts
- Building RSO exam study tools or flashcard sets
- Producing merit badge or rank materials tied to shooting sports
- Answering questions about Webelos/AOL shooting sports rank requirements
- Any document related to NCS Shooting Sports Director qualification

## Inputs

Confirm before starting:
1. Discipline in scope: rifle, shotgun, archery, or all three
2. Audience: Scouts (specify rank), leaders, or NRA instructor candidates
3. Deliverable format: Word document, interactive widget, or plain script
4. Task type: certification planning, range operations, or curriculum

Default audience if unspecified: Scouts BSA youth, Troop 3, Marianna FL.

## Procedure

### NRA Certification Path (follow in order, no skipping)

1. Confirm NRA Basic Instructor Training (BIT) complete — prerequisite for all discipline certs.
2. Rifle: NRA Rifle Instructor certification.
3. Shotgun: NRA Shotgun Instructor certification.
4. Archery: USA Archery Level 1 Instructor Certification (online — separate from NRA path).
5. After all three: apply for NCS Shooting Sports Director course via Alaflo Council.
6. Before NCS enrollment confirm: current adult BSA membership, YPT current, Annual Health and Medical Record complete.

Always verify course dates and registration windows with Alaflo Council shooting sports committee.

### Range Safety Script (required elements — all mandatory)

1. Equipment check: eye protection, ear protection, range flags.
2. State the three safety rules verbatim:
   - Always keep the gun pointed in a safe direction.
   - Always keep your finger off the trigger until ready to shoot.
   - Always keep the gun unloaded until ready to use.
3. Range-specific rules: no aerial targets, no ground targets (ricochet), shoot own target only, no straddling the firing line.
4. Tiger rank: adult partner present at all times. Hard requirement — never omit.
5. Firing line commands — include expected response for each:
   - "Is the line ready?" — thumbs up if ready
   - "The line is ready." — stay in position
   - "Load." — cock and load
   - "Ready on the firing line." — final position, finger off trigger
   - "Commence firing." — begin shooting
   - "Cease firing — clear all guns." — stop, show clear, set gun down
   - "Leave the firing line." — step back
   - "Go forward, retrieve targets." — move downrange only on this command
6. Cease Fire authority: anyone may call it. Commence Fire: Rangemaster only.
7. Comprehension check before opening the range — minimum three questions. Do not open until every Scout answers correctly.

Webelos and AOL Scouts must explain commands back to the Rangemaster — this is a rank requirement. State it explicitly.

### RSO Study Materials

1. Source: publicly surfaced NRA RSO exam questions.
2. Organize by category: safety rules, range commands, emergency procedures, legal liability.
3. Build interactive flashcard or quiz format when possible.
4. Include rationale with answers, not just correct responses.

## Output Format

- Range safety scripts: Word document (.docx), section headers, command table, comprehension check section.
- Certification checklists: Word document or plain checklist, one item per checkbox.
- RSO study tools: interactive HTML widget or structured Q&A document.
- NCS planning: Word document with timeline, prerequisites checklist, council contact notes.

All documents: clear section headers, active voice, short sentences. No filler language.

## Quality Checks

Range safety documents — verify before delivering:
- Three safety rules stated verbatim and in order.
- Tiger rank adult-partner requirement present.
- Cease Fire / Commence Fire authority explicitly distinguished.
- Comprehension check section included with minimum three questions.
- Webelos/AOL rank requirement stated.

Certification checklists — verify before delivering:
- BIT listed as first prerequisite.
- USA Archery Level 1 listed separately from NRA certs.
- BSA membership, YPT, and medical record listed as NCS enrollment requirements.
